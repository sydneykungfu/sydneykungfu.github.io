Kung Fu Sydney – Class Content & Syllabus

Forms are not the objective; they are vehicles. There are a lot of san sau, drills, and pad works in between. Explore the syllabus to understand the progression and techniques taught at Tse Kung Fu Academy.

Classes structure will includes:

  • Traditional Choy Lee Fut Forms.
  • Application Drills, San Sau - Understand the usage. Self-defence.
  • Pad Works - Accuracy and Timing.
  • Conditioning - Strength and Flexibility.
  • Light Sparring - Intermediate Level or above.

Our Kung Fu classes in Sydney are designed for all levels, from beginners to advanced practitioners. We embrased small class, hand by hand training, and personal attention to ensure that each student receives the guidance they need to progress at their own pace. We focus on practical self-defense techniques, traditional forms, and physical conditioning.

We offer an optional ranking / sash system for students who wish to progress in their Kung Fu journey.

Syllabus Core

🟢 Beginner Level (No Sash)

Objective: Learn fundamental movements, coordination, and conditioning.

  • 五輪馬 – Ng Lun Ma (ńgh lèuhn máh)
    English: Five Wheel Horse Stance
    A foot work exercise to develop balance and stability. It involves shifting weight between the legs while maintaining a low stance.
  • 五輪拳 – Ng Lun Kyun (ńgh lèuhn kyùhn)
    English: Five Wheel Fist
    A basic punching technique that emphasizes proper alignment and body mechanics. It involves a series of punches delivered in a circular motion.

🟡 Foundation Level (Yellow Sash)

Objective: Build strong foundational power (Long Bridge Power) and introduce basic combat techniques.

  • 小十字拳 – Siu Sap Ji Kyun (síu sahp jih kyùhn)
    English: Small Cross Fist
  • 小梅花拳 – Siu Miu Fa Kyun (síu mòuh fàa kyùhn)
    English: Small Plum Blossom Fist

🔴 Intermediate Level (Red Sash)

Objective: One-to-one and multiple attacker combat. Improve long power and begin short power.

  • 車斬滾 – Che Jaam Gwan (chē jáam gwán)
    English: Sweeping Rolling Strike
  • 十字扣打 – Sap Ji Kau Da (sahp jih kau dáa)
    English: Cross Trap and Strike
  • 雙頭棍 – Seung Tau Gwan (sēung tàuh gwan )
    English: Double-Headed Staff
  • 單刀 – Daan Dou (dāan dōu)
    English: Single Broadsword

⚫ Advanced Level (Black Sash)

Objective: Refine long and short power. Master multiple-opponent combat.

  • 脫戰拳 – Tyut Jin Kyun (tyut jin kyùhn)
    English: Breakaway Combat Fist
  • 金豹平肘拳 – Gam Paau Ping Jau Kyun (gām paau pìhng jáu kyùhn)
    English: Golden Leopard Level Elbow Fist
  • 佛家掌 – Fat Gaa Jeung (fàht gā jéung)
    English: Buddhist Palm
  • 單頭棍 – Daan Tau Gwan (dāan tàuh gwan)
    English: Single-End Staff

⚪ Elective / Non-Core Forms

Objective: Explore additional forms and techniques to enhance skills and understanding.

  • 梅花槍 – Miu Fa Cheung (mòuh fàa chèung)
    English: Plum Blossom Spear
  • 白模拳 – Baak Mou Kyun (baahk mòuh kyùhn)
    English: White Imitation Fist
  • 行者棒 – Hang Je Paang (hàhng jé páang)
    English: Wanderer's Staff
  • 鞭橋 – Bin Kiu (bīn kìuh)
    English: Whipping Bridge
  • 春秋大刀 – Cheun Chau Daai Dou (chēun chāu daaih dōu)
    English: Spring-Autumn Kwan Dao
  • 木人樁 – Muk Yan Jong (muhk yàhn jōng)
    English: Wooden Dummy
