Syllabus Core

🟢 Beginner Level

Objective: Learn fundamental movements, coordination, and conditioning.

  • 五輪馬 – Five Wheel Horse Stance
    Cantonese: ńgh lèuhn máh (Ng Lun Ma)
    A foot work exercise to develop balance and stability. It involves shifting weight between the legs while maintaining a low stance.
  • 五輪拳 – Five Wheel Fist
    Cantonese: ńgh lèuhn kyùhn (Ng Lun Kyun)
    A basic punching technique that emphasizes proper alignment and body mechanics. It involves a series of punches delivered in a circular motion.

🟡 Foundation Level

Objective: Build strong foundational power (Long Bridge Power) and introduce basic combat techniques.

  • 小十字拳 – Small Cross Fist
    Cantonese: síu sahp jih kyùhn (Siu Sap Ji Kyun)
  • 小梅花拳 – Small Plum Blossom Fist
    Cantonese: síu mòuh fàa kyùhn (Siu Miu Fa Kyun)

🔴 Intermediate Level

Objective: One-to-one and multiple attacker combat. Improve long power and begin short power.

  • 車斬滾 – Sweeping Rolling Strike
    Cantonese: chē jáam gwán (Che Jaam Gwan)
  • 十字扣打 – Cross Trap and Strike
    Cantonese: sahp jih kau dáa (Sap Ji Kau Da)
  • 雙頭棍 – Double-Headed Staff
    Cantonese: sēung tàuh gwan (Seung Tau Gwan)
  • 單刀 – Single Broadsword
    Cantonese: dāan dōu (Daan Dou)

⚫ Advanced Level

Objective: Refine long and short power. Master multiple-opponent combat.

  • 脫戰拳 – Breakaway Combat Fist
    Cantonese: tyut jin kyùhn (Tyut Jin Kyun)
  • 金豹平肘拳 – Golden Leopard Level Elbow Fist
    Cantonese: gām paau pìhng jáu kyùhn (Gam Paau Ping Jau Kyun)
  • 佛家掌 – Buddhist Palm
    Cantonese: fàht gā jéung (Fat Gaa Jeung)
  • 單頭棍 – Single-End Staff
    Cantonese: dāan tàuh gwan (Daan Tau Gwan)

⚪ Elective / Non-Core Forms

Objective: Explore additional forms and techniques to enhance skills and understanding.

  • 梅花槍 – Plum Blossom Spear
    Cantonese: mòuh fàa chèung (Miu Fa Cheung)
  • 白模拳 – White Imitation Fist
    Cantonese: baahk mòuh kyùhn (Baak Mou Kyun)
  • 行者棒 – Wanderer’s Staff
    Cantonese: hàhng jé páang (Hang Je Paang)
  • 鞭橋 – Whipping Bridge
    Cantonese: bīn kìuh (Bin Kiu)
  • 春秋大刀 – Spring-Autumn Kwan Dao
    Cantonese: chēun chāu daaih dōu (Cheun Chau Daai Dou)
  • 木人樁 – Wooden Dummy
    Cantonese: muhk yàhn jōng (Muk Yan Jong)
