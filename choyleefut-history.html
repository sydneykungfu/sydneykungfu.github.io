The History of Choy Lee Fut

Choy Lee Fut 「蔡李佛」 (also spelled as Choy Lay Fut or Choy Li Fut) is a traditional Chinese martial art founded in 1836 by Chan Heung 陳享. It combines the techniques of Southern and Northern Chinese martial arts, making it a versatile and effective system for self-defense, health, and fitness.

Origins

The name "Choy Lee Fut" 「蔡李佛」 honors Chan Heung's teachers: Choy Fook 蔡福, Lee Yau San 李友山, and Chan Heung's uncle Chan Yuen-Woo 陳遠護. The system incorporates the rapid hand techniques of Choy Fook, the powerful stances of Lee Yau San, and the agility and flexibility of Chan Yuen-Woo's teachings.

Philosophy

Choy Lee Fut emphasizes the balance between physical training and mental discipline. It teaches respect, humility, and perseverance, values that are deeply rooted in Chinese culture.

Global Influence

Today, Choy Lee Fut is practiced worldwide and is recognized for its effectiveness in combat and its health benefits. It remains a cornerstone of traditional Chinese martial arts.
